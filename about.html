Jim Creel

Experience

Assistant Professor of English

University of Wyoming

2015-2023

After completing a Ph.D. in Rhetoric and Composition at TCU, I went to work for UW as a tenure-track assistant professor. I taught courses in technical writing - including Writing for Tech and Sciences - and developed courses and curriculum including video game design, film, and cultural studies. I wrote grants to build video game design labs and to develop a new minor in Film Studies. I also built and maintained a digital film archive so that students during the COVID-19 pandemic would have access to films for their courses. During this time, I also published research in rhetoric, cultural studies, video games, and film studies. My work appears in The Games Culture Reader, The Journal for Popular Culture, and at The Journal of Multimodal Rhetorics

Software Engineer

After eight years of teaching and writing, I realized that I wanted a more concrete work pattern with tangible outcomes. I decided to pursue a career in software engineering and taught myself Python, starting with web scrapers that sent text and email notifications for limited edition merchandise online. I branched out at the advice of my brother, teaching myself JavaScript and building my first full-stack web application using Javascript and Python to check Disney Parks reservations and send notifications when dates were available. I then enrolled in General Assembly's Software Engineering Immersive program, where I used my final projects to really stretch my skills by taking on large, complex datasets. I graduated in May 2023, and am looking for opportunities that will benefit from my ability to learn quickly and adapt to new situations.
